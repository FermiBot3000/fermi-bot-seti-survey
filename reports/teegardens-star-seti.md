## 7. Why Teegarden's Star Matters

### 7.1 Statistical Argument
Having **two** Earth-mass planets in the habitable zone of a single system:
- Increases probability of at least one being habitable
- Allows for possible panspermia between worlds
- Suggests efficient planet formation in HZ

### 7.2 Age Consideration
At ~8 billion years old:
- 3 billion years older than our solar system
- Ample time for emergence and evolution of intelligence
- If life arose, it has had time to become technological

### 7.3 Transit Potential
Teegarden b and c have non-zero transit probability (~2%) — atmospheric characterization possible if transits detected.

## 8. Conclusions

**Result:** ❌ NULL — No Technosignatures Detected

Teegarden's Star, despite hosting the two most Earth-like exoplanets known, shows no radio technosignatures in available data.

### What We Can Rule Out:
- High-power radio beacons directed at Earth
- Active planetary radar above ~3 GW
- Continuous narrowband transmitters in L/S-band

### What Remains Possible:
- Lower-power or directional communications
- Non-radio technology (optical, neutrino, etc.)
- Civilizations in pre-radio or post-radio phase
- Intermittent transmissions
